Executive Summary

Government contracting has long been inefficient, and workforce growth has not kept pace with procurement spending in recent years. In FY22, a single federal contracting officer had to execute an average of 2,000 contracting actions per year, compared to only 300 in 2013 (Deloitte). AI-driven automation has huge potential to reshape this landscape.

Procurement Sciences is an organization at the forefront of the shift. In this report, Anthony Brown, Former Head of AI and Data at Procurement Sciences, shares the technical innovations and system architecture behind their approach, offering insights for tech leaders looking to implement AI in high-stakes, regulatory-driven environments.

We’ve seen RFIs generated – the entire package, sometimes consisting of 20 or more documents – in just 15 minutes. Previously, completing such tasks could take up to six months.”

– Anthony Brown

The problem

Manual workflows, prolonged timelines, and high compliance risks have made the Request for Proposal (RFP) and Request for Information (RFI) processes notoriously complex and time-consuming. Notably, they involve processing large amounts of solicitation data and writing a response proposal that meets all of a given agency’s requirements.

The solution

The Procurement Sciences Awarded AI platform uses domain-specific AI assistants, multi-agent orchestration, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to streamline the end-to-end contracting process. The platform:

  • Automates solicitation analysis
  • Generates tailored, high-quality proposals efficiently
  • Ensures compliance with evolving regulations

The result is dramatically improved speed, accuracy, and compliance.

Result metrics

Clients have seen an average 90% efficiency increase across planning and proposal generation stages.

Key decisions

  • Don’t reinvent the wheel: Outsource to secure cloud infrastructure providers to provide compliance assurance
  • Acknowledge and adapt to the strengths and weaknesses of different LLM models
  • Bake transparency and traceability into the product for risk-averse clients
