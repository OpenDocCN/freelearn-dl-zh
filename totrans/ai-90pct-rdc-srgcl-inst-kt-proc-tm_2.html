Executive Summary

Medical supply chains were thrust onto the global stage during the COVID-19 pandemic, when PPE shortages hit many areas of the world. This report looks at how Medloaner, a Florida-based startup headed by Ukrainian Kip Pogrebenko, navigated the fragmented and heavily regulated healthcare landscape.

We focus on the decisions and insights involved in developing and deploying an AI-assisted automated solution for medical device reprocessing, supporting mission-critical links in the supply chain. Kip’s awareness of the importance of personal relationships and identifying internal champions is a takeaway for all tech leaders in healthcare.

The #1 lesson: if you want to build something for healthcare, you have to forget about any virality that would improve your growth. It doesn’t exist. It’s all based on personal relationships and it’s highly correlated with the technology adoption lifecycle.”

– Kip Pogrebenko, CEO and Co-Founder

Medloaner has begun its journey with partners in Canada. It is planning an expansion to the Middle East, Africa, and eventually the US.

In this report, we focus on Medloaner’s solution for its Canadian design partner, Hamilton Health Sciences (HHS), and a group of four of its hospitals.

Core challenge

Launching AI products in regulated industries involves complex hurdles:

  • Ensuring compliance with privacy laws like HIPAA
  • Managing sensitive or restricted data while maintaining operational effectiveness
  • Balancing the demands of innovation with the necessity for trust and reliability

Medloaner’s example demonstrates a way to confront these challenges head-on while delivering measurable operational improvements.

Problem and solution outline

Problem context: The manual reprocessing of surgical kits is time-intensive and error-prone. It includes the tracking, cleaning, inspecting, and assembling of kits. Regulatory demands and operational inefficiencies compound these challenges, with 58% of surgical delays tied to equipment issues (a stat on a previous version of Medloaner’s own website).

Solution overview: Medloaner’s AI-powered system automates reprocessing workflows, reducing time per kit from 10 minutes to about one minute. Using a combination of computer vision and AI-driven analytics, the solution offers visual instructions for technicians, verifies correct kit assembly, and identifies errors early to avoid costly re-sterilization cycles.

Regulatory approach: From the outset, Medloaner prioritized compliance by isolating its system from patient data, focusing instead on procedural and operational workflows. This separation minimized exposure to HIPAA-related risks while preserving the system’s utility.

Key decisions

The plays that define the Medloaner strategy to date:

  • Focusing on internal champions
  • Due diligence around patient data concerns
  • Avoiding the use of patient data
  • Understanding user personas
  • Analyzing competitor failures closely and adapting in response

Frameworks used by Medloaner

  • The technology adoption lifecycle
  • Porter’s Five Forces
  • Land and expand growth strategy
  • Network effects
