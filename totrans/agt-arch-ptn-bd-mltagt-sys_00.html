Preface

Generative AI, and specifically agentic AI, is a paradigm that is rapidly becoming a pillar of modern enterprise technology. Agentic AI entails designing systems where large language models (LLMs) act not just as monolithic text generators, but as distributed reasoning engines, each capable of perceiving their environment, making decisions, and taking actions to collectively achieve specific goals. Among other advantages, agentic AI allows the automation of complex, cognitive workflows. This reduces the need for human intervention in multi-step processes and thus enables the creation of adaptive, autonomous systems that can scale expertise across an organization while integrating human approval in the processes that are most critical.

Among all the emerging technologies in this space, there are many frameworks and their abstractions as patterns that allow for the creation of these agents. This book focuses on the universal architectural patterns that underpin robust agentic systems, while utilizing powerful tools like Google’s Gemini models and the Agent Development Kit (ADK), along with popular frameworks like LangGraph and CrewAI. These tools are pivotal today because, in addition to being at the cutting edge of the industry, they offer the following advantages:

  • They allow for the creation of agents that can reason, plan, and use tools to interact with the real world.
  • They support complex coordination between multiple agents, considering dependencies and shared context.
  • They provide the infrastructure for governing, securing, and observing autonomous systems in production.

In this book dedicated to designing and building progressively more sophisticated agentic AI, we will first discuss the landscape of generative AI in the enterprise, the selection and adaptation of "agent-ready" LLMs, how to architect the anatomy of an intelligent agent, and how to govern and integrate these systems using a maturity model.

Once the foundational concepts and agent anatomy are understood, we will discuss the practical design patterns for building robust systems that combine agents. We will explore how to coordinate multiple agents, ensure explainability and compliance, implement fault tolerance, and design effective human-agent interactions.

Finally, we will conclude this book by looking at advanced implementations and real-world use cases, including building self-improving systems, a detailed progressive roadmap for enterprise adoption, and practical coding examples of single and multi-agent systems for financial workflows using modern frameworks.

This book will guide you through a comprehensive pattern language for designing and building agentic systems, and it will also cover practical recipes on integrating and implementing these patterns with tools such as Google ADK, CrewAI, and LangGraph.

Most of the agent configurations described in this book are illustrated using Google’s Gemini models and Python, but you can apply these architectural patterns to other LLMs and frameworks.

In this book, I wanted to share our experience of real and practical agentic ai scenarios that we have encountered while working with enterprises and engaging with the global AI community.

Who this book is for

This book is for software architects, senior developers, AI engineers, and technical leaders looking to move beyond simple chatbot prototypes and build robust, production-grade agentic systems. Experience with Python programming, basic machine learning concepts, and a familiarity with API-based development is required to get the most out of this agentic AI book.

What this book covers

Chapter 1, GenAI in the Enterprise: Landscape, Maturity, and Agent Focus, details the transformative potential of GenAI, introduces the core concept of agentic systems, and presents the GenAI Maturity Model as a strategic roadmap for adoption.

Chapter 2, Agent-Ready LLMs: Selection, Deployment, and Adaptation, introduces criteria for selecting the right foundation models, strategies for efficient deployment and serving, and the discipline of AgentOps for managing the agent life cycle.

Chapter 3, The Spectrum of LLM Adaptation for Agents: RAG to Fine-tuning, explores techniques for specializing LLMs, ranging from dynamic context retrieval (RAG) and in-context learning to parameter-efficient fine-tuning.

Chapter 4, Agentic AI Architecture: Components and Interactions, dissects the fundamental anatomy of an agent—Sense, Reason, Plan, Act—and examines the architectural features and data contexts required for agents to operate intelligently.

Chapter 5, Multi-Agent Coordination Patterns, covers strategies for managing collaboration between agents, including the Agent Router, task delegation frameworks (Supervisor vs. Swarm), and patterns for negotiation and consensus.

Chapter 6, Explainability and Compliance Agentic Patterns, details patterns for ensuring accountability, such as Instruction Fidelity Auditing and Fractal Chain-of-Thought (FCoT) embedding, to create transparent and auditable reasoning trails.

Chapter 7, Robustness and Fault Tolerance Patterns, introduces architectural safeguards like Adaptive Retry, Circuit Breakers, and Parallel Execution Consensus to ensure agents remain reliable in the face of errors and uncertainty.

Chapter 8, Human-Agent Interaction Patterns, examines the interface between AI and people, covering patterns for delegation, seamless handoffs, and Human-in-the-Loop escalation strategies for high-stakes decisions.

Chapter 9, Agent-Level Patterns, focuses on the internal capabilities of a single agent, exploring patterns for managing agent-specific memory, structured reasoning, and multimodal perception.

Chapter 10, System-Level Patterns for Production Readiness, discusses the holistic infrastructure required for enterprise deployment, including patterns for security, discovery registries, and event-driven reactivity.

Chapter 11, Advanced Adaptation: Building Agents That Learn, explores the cutting edge of self-improving systems, covering synthetic data generation, automated scoring, and the "flywheel" effect of co-evolved agent training.

Chapter 12, A Practical Roadmap: Implementing Agentic Patterns by Maturity Level, provides a synthesized guide for progressively adopting these patterns, mapping them to specific stages of organizational maturity from foundational to advanced.

Chapter 13, Use Case: A Single Agent for Loan Processing, walks through a practical implementation of a monolithic agent handling a complex financial workflow, demonstrating the application of FCoT and tool use in code.

Chapter 14, Use Case: A Multi-Agent System for Loan Processing, evolves the previous use case into a distributed multi-agent system, showcasing how specialized agents collaborate to handle intake, risk, and compliance.

Chapter 15, Agent Frameworks – Use Case: A Multi-Agent System for Loan Processing with CrewAI and LangGraph, compares and contrasts popular industry frameworks by re-implementing the loan processing scenario to highlight the strengths of different abstraction models.

Chapter 16, Conclusion: Charting Your Agentic AI Journey, summarizes the key architectural principles, offers a progressively more sophisticated practitioner's roadmap and action plan, and provides a final perspective on the future of the agentic workforce.
