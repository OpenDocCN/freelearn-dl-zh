Part 1

Foundations and Core Agent Concepts

In this part, you will build the strategic and technical foundation required to transition from simple GenAI experiments to robust, production-grade agentic systems. We begin by exploring the GenAI Maturity Model, a roadmap that charts the evolution from basic data preparation to advanced multi-agent collaboration. You will dissect the fundamental anatomy of an agent—Sense, Reason, Plan, and Act—and introduce the emerging agentic stack, including interoperability standards like the Model Context Protocol (MCP) and Agent-to-Agent (A2A) communication. Finally, we focus on the agent’s cognitive core, detailing how to select, deploy, and adapt LLMs using techniques ranging from retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to fine-tuning, ensuring your models are truly "agent-ready."

This part of the book includes the following chapters:

  • Chapter 1, GenAI in the Enterprise: Landscape, Maturity, and Agent Focus
  • Chapter 2, Agent-Ready LLMs: Selection, Deployment, and Adaptation
  • Chapter 3, The Spectrum of LLM Adaptation for Agents: RAG to Fine-tuning
